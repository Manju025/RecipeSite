Tomato Rice

Tomato Rice is a simple South Indian meal where rice is cooked with lots of tomatoes, fresh herbs, whole spices & ground spices. It tastes simply delicious, aromatic and makes for a quick meal option any time of the year. It is not a traditional dish so each Indian home and region has a different way of making it.

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 medium-sized onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, mined
  • 2-3 medium-sized tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala (or curry powder)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (cilantro), chopped for garnish
Optional: You can add vegetables like peas, carrots, or bell peppers to enhance the dish's nutritional value.

Steps:

  • Rinse the rice under running water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes. Drain the water before cooking.
  • In a large pan or pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  • Add the minced garlic and ginger to the onions and sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.
  • Now, add the chopped tomatoes to the pan and cook them until they become soft and mushy, and the oil starts to separate.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala (or curry powder), and salt to the tomato mixture. Stir well and let the spices cook for a couple of minutes to enhance the flavors.
  • If you are adding vegetables, this is the time to add them to the pan. Cook for a few minutes until the vegetables become slightly tender.
  • Add the soaked and drained rice to the pan. Gently stir to combine the rice with the tomato and spice mixture, ensuring the rice is coated well.
  • Pour the water into the pan and give everything a good mix. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  • Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pan with a lid, and let the rice simmer until it's cooked completely and the water is absorbed. This will take about 15-20 minutes.
  • Once the rice is cooked, turn off the heat and let it rest for 5 minutes. Fluff the rice gently with a fork.
  • Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves (cilantro) for added freshness and aroma..

Your delicious and flavorful tomato rice is ready to be served! Enjoy it as a standalone dish or pair it with raita (yogurt sauce) for a delightful meal.

Lentil vegetable soup

Lentils are rich in fibre, folate and potassium making them a great choice for the heart and for managing blood pressure and cholesterol. They are also a source of energising iron and vitamin B1 which helps maintain a steady heartbeat.

  • 1 cup dried lentils (green or brown), rinsed and picked through
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 bell pepper (any color), diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes (or use fresh tomatoes, chopped)
  • 6 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped, for garnish
  • Lemon wedges (optional, for serving)

Steps:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent.
  2. Add the minced garlic to the pot and sauté for another minute until the garlic releases its aroma.
  3. Stir in the diced carrots, celery, bell pepper, and zucchini. Cook the vegetables for a few minutes until they begin to soften.
  4. Add the rinsed lentils to the pot, along with the diced tomatoes (with their juice). Stir everything together.
  5. Pour in the vegetable broth or water to the pot. Add ground cumin, ground coriander, paprika, bay leaf, salt, and pepper. Give it a good stir to combine all the ingredients.
  6. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes or until the lentils and vegetables are tender.
  7. Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning as needed. You can add more salt, pepper, or spices to suit your taste.
  8. Once the lentils and vegetables are cooked to your liking, turn off the heat. Remove the bay leaf and discard it.
  9. Ladle the Lentil Vegetable Soup into bowls, and garnish with chopped fresh parsley or cilantro.
  10. Optional: Serve the soup with lemon wedges on the side. Squeezing some fresh lemon juice into the soup adds a bright and tangy flavor.
