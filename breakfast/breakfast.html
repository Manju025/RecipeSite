Idli

a type of savoury rice cake, originating from South India, popular as a breakfast food in Southern India and in Sri Lanka. The cakes are made by steaming a batter consisting of fermented black lentils (de-husked) and rice.

  • 1 cup idli rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi)
  • Salt to taste

Steps:

  1. Wash the idli rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately under running water.
  2. In a large bowl, soak the idli rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds in water separately. Allow them to soak for 4-6 hours. The soaking process is essential for soft and fluffy idlis.
  3. After soaking, drain the water from the rice and dal.
  4. In a blender or wet grinder, grind the soaked urad dal and fenugreek seeds together to make a smooth paste. Use as little water as possible while grinding.
  5. Next, grind the soaked idli rice separately to a slightly coarse consistency. The batter should be smooth but not too fine.
  6. Mix the ground urad dal and rice batter together in a large bowl. Add salt to taste and mix well.
  7. Cover the bowl with a lid and allow the batter to ferment overnight or for about 8-10 hours. Fermentation is crucial for making soft and fluffy idlis.
  8. The next morning, you will notice that the batter has risen and is airy due to fermentation.
  9. Grease the idli plates with oil or cooking spray to prevent sticking.
  10. Pour the batter into each mold of the idli plates, filling them about three-fourths full.
  11. Steam the idlis in a steamer for about 10-12 minutes on medium heat or until a toothpick inserted into the center of an idli comes out clean. Avoid overcooking to maintain the softness of the idlis.
  12. Once the idlis are cooked, remove them from the steamer and let them cool for a minute.
  13. Use a spoon or a butter knife to gently remove the idlis from the molds.
  14. Serve the hot and fluffy idlis with coconut chutney, sambar, or any other accompaniment of your choice.

Enjoy your homemade Idlis!

Hot Drinks

Tea

People all over the world have been drinking tea for thousands of centuries, and for good reason. Numerous studies have shown that a variety of teas may boost your immune system, fight off inflammation, and even ward off cancer and heart disease.

  • water
  • Tea leaves or tea bags(Black tea or your preferred type)
  • Milk
  • Sugar

Steps:

  1. Boil Water:

    In a saucepan or kettle, add the desired amount of water based on the number of cups of tea you want to make. Place the saucepan on the stove and bring the water to a rolling boil.

  2. Add Tea Leaves or Tea Bags:

    Once the water reaches a boil, turn off the heat. If you're using loose tea leaves, add them directly to the hot water. For tea bags, simply place the tea bag(s) into the water.

  3. Steep the Tea:

    Let the tea steep in the hot water for about 2 to 5 minutes, depending on how strong you like your tea. The longer you steep, the stronger the flavor will be.

  4. Add Milk:

    If you prefer to have milk in your tea, you can add it during or after steeping. You can use dairy milk, soy milk, almond milk, or any milk substitute of your choice. Stir in the desired amount of milk to achieve your preferred tea color and taste.

  5. Add Sugar:

    If you like your tea sweet, you can add sugar, honey, or any other sweetener while the tea is still hot. Adjust the sweetness to your taste.

  6. Strain (If Using Loose Tea Leaves):

    If you like your tea sweet, you can add sugar, honey, or any other sweetener while the tea is still hot. Adjust the sweetness to your taste.

  7. Serve:

    Pour the tea into your favorite teacup and enjoy!
