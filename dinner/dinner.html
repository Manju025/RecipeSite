Chicken Biryani

The reason why chicken biryani is so famous is due to the different flavours and the ingredients it carries as well as the nostalgia it evokes as a royal and iconic dish. One of the most common ways to prepare chicken biryani is to layer spiced chicken followed by rice, fried onions, and ghee.

  • Marinating the Chicken:
    • In a large bowl, mix the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, biryani masala, salt, and lemon juice.
    • Cover the bowl and let the chicken marinate for at least 1-2 hours in the refrigerator (for best results, marinate overnight).
  • Preparing the Biryani Rice:
    • In a large pot, bring plenty of water to a boil. Add the soaked and drained basmati rice, along with the whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, green cardamoms, cinnamon).
    • Cook the rice until it is about 70-80% cooked (still slightly firm). Drain the water and set the rice aside.
  • Assembling the Biryani:
    • In a large heavy-bottomed pot or biryani pot, heat some ghee or oil. Layer half of the marinated chicken at the bottom of the pot.
    • Add a layer of half-cooked rice on top of the chicken.
    • Sprinkle some chopped fresh coriander leaves and mint leaves on the rice.
    • Repeat the layers with the remaining chicken and rice, finishing with a layer of rice on top.
    • Drizzle the saffron-infused milk over the top layer of rice. Also, add the fried onions (if using) for added flavor and garnish.
    • Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can also seal the lid with dough to trap the steam (traditional dum cooking method) or use a heavy pan or tava under the pot to prevent direct contact with the heat.
    • Cook the biryani on low heat for about 25-30 minutes or until the chicken is fully cooked and the rice is tender and fluffy.
  • Serving the Hyderabadi Chicken Biryani:
    • Gently fluff the biryani with a fork, being careful not to break the rice grains.
    • Serve the biryani hot, garnished with chopped fresh coriander leaves and mint leaves.

Veg Paneer Biryani

A traditional Paneer Biryani recipe is a slow cooked layered casserole of paneer cubes in a curd (yogurt) based gravy, fried onions and par-cooked rice flavored with spices, saffron or rose water. Any traditionally made biryani will have separate layers of the gravy and rice, utilizing the dum method of cooking.

For the Biryani Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked in water for 30 minutes and drained
  • Whole spices (1 bay leaf, 4-5 cloves, 4-5 green cardamoms, 1-inch cinnamon stick)
  • Salt to taste

For the Paneer and Veggie Mixture:

  • 200g paneer, cubed
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, etc.), chopped
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt (curd)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh coriander leaves (cilantro) and mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup fried onions (optional, for garnish)
  • Ghee (clarified butter) or vegetable oil for cooking
  • Salt to taste

Steps:

  1. For the Biryani Rice:
    • In a large pot, bring plenty of water to a boil. Add the soaked and drained basmati rice, along with the whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, green cardamoms, cinnamon), and salt to taste.
    • Cook the rice until it is about 70-80% cooked (still slightly firm). Drain the water and set the rice aside.
  2. For the Paneer and Veggie Mixture:
    • In a pan, heat some ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the thinly sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
    • Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.
    • Mix in the mixed vegetables and cook for a few minutes until they become slightly tender.
    • Add the paneer cubes to the pan and gently toss everything together.
    • Stir in the turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt to taste. Mix well.
    • Add the plain yogurt to the pan and stir until the paneer and vegetables are coated with the yogurt and spices.
    • Finally, add the chopped fresh coriander leaves and mint leaves to the mixture. Mix everything together and turn off the heat.
  3. Assembling the Paneer Veg Biryani:
    • In a large heavy-bottomed pot or biryani pot, layer half of the partially cooked rice at the bottom.
    • Add a layer of the paneer and veggie mixture on top of the rice.
    • Continue with another layer of rice, followed by the rest of the paneer and veggie mixture.
    • Finish with a final layer of rice on top.
    • Optionally, garnish with fried onions for added flavor and aroma.
    • Drizzle some ghee on top of the final rice layer to keep the biryani moist and flavorful.
  4. Dum Cooking (slow cooking on low heat):
    • Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can also seal the lid with dough to trap the steam (traditional dum cooking method) or use a heavy pan or tava under the pot to prevent direct contact with the heat.
    • Cook the biryani on very low heat for about 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together and the rice to cook fully.
  5. Serving the Paneer Veg Biryani:
    • Gently fluff the biryani with a fork, being careful not to break the rice grains.
    • Serve the Paneer Veg Biryani hot, garnished with chopped fresh coriander leaves and fried onions (if using).
